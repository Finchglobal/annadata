Project Annadata: Bridging the 16% Gap
Implementation Plan for Annadata Impact Credits (AIC)
1. Executive Summary
The Indian agricultural sector remains the backbone of the nation, yet the primary
provider—the Annadata—receives less than 16% of the final consumer value of their
produce. This project introduces Annadata Impact Credits (AIC), a revolutionary
financial instrument designed to recognize and reward the unrecognized hardship,
social contribution, and environmental stewardship of farmers. 
By leveraging the SIR (Social Impact Rewards) framework and a high-performance
"Antigravity" tech stack, this plan outlines the transition of 150,000 farmers into a
system where impact is verified via satellite and rewarded with direct, absolute
financial values. 
2. Data Acquisition Strategy
The system utilizes a Hybrid Verification Model to minimize farmer friction while
maximizing auditability.
Category A: Social & Demographic (Farmer Input)
Collected via a lightweight mobile interface to assess the social equity impact:
Family Composition: Total members and dependency ratios.
Gender Focus: Number of female members and unmarried girl children (key
indicators for ESG-linked educational and health rewards).
Category B: Financial Baseline (Farmer Input)
Yearly Yield (Absolute INR): Used to calculate the current "Value Gap" between
farm-gate prices and market value.

3. The AIC Calculation Formula
The reward value is not a mere percentage; it is an absolute reward based on a
composite impact score.
AIC = (Area × [Wsocial + Wregen]) + (Yield × Gap_Factor)
Wsocial: Weighted multiplier for female dependency and social hardship.
Wregen: Environmental score derived from Google Earth Engine satellite data.
Gap_Factor: Coefficient designed to recover the unrecognized 84% value lost in the supply chain. 
4. Local Governance & Validation
The Ward-Level Protocol: To ensure "Bulletproof" data, the Panchayati Raj structure is
integrated. Digital verification is triggered at the Ward Level, where the elected Ward Member
provides social verification of the farmer’s identity and hardship, acting as a human layer of trust
over the satellite data.

5. Technical Architecture (Antigravity Stack)
Frontend: Next.js (Vercel) – A high-speed, mobile-optimized dashboard for
farmers and validators.
Database & Auth: Supabase (PostgreSQL) – Utilizing PostGIS to store farm
polygons and Row Level Security for data protection.
Verification Engine: Supabase Edge Functions integrated with Google Earth
Engine API for automated satellite monitoring.
Payouts: Automated absolute reward distribution via UPI-linked SIR gateways.
